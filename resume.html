Education

School - 1978 to 1991

Before attending UWA, I spent time at Beehive Montessori, Nedlands Primary, and Hollywood High schools. I took the standard subjects for someone intending to study a science-based subject at UWA: physics, chemistry, the two TEE math units, and English Literature. Social sciences, photography, and cooking rounded things out, from what I remember.

UWA (BSc. Hons) - 1992 to 1996

I spent 1992 to 1996 completing a science degree at UWA. My time there was split between Linguistics, Mathematics, and Computer Science. While completing enough units for a major in pure mathematics, my official majors were in Linguistics and Computing, and honours was split between the two.

UWA (BSc.) Mathematics

  • 1992 – Mathematics and Math Sciences (Roughly: pure vs applied)
  • 1993 – Mathematics and Math Sciences, Analysis (2P2), Algebra & Discrete Mathematics
  • 1994 – Topics in Algebra (groups, rings, etc.), Combinatorics and Geometry
  • 1995 – Math Sciences, Number Theory and its Application, Robotics.

UWA (BSc.) Linguistics

  • 1992 – Linguistics 100 (general intro)
  • 1993 – Linguistics 252 & 253 (Computational and something else?)
  • 1993 – Linguistics 310 & 399 (Australian languages and historical?)
  • 1994 – Topics in Algebra (groups, rings, etc.), Combinatorics and Geometry
  • 1995 – Math Sciences, Number Theory and its Applications, Robotics.

UWA (BSc.) Computing

  • 1992 – Programming Techniques (procedural), Applicative Programming (logic & functional)
  • 1993 – Data Structures, Operating Systems, Computer Systems Programming
  • 1994 – Algorithms, Computer Networks
  • 1995 – Concurrent Programming, Programming Language Implementation
  • 1996 – Honours Project – Parsing Warpiri using HPSG.

UWA (Extension) – 1997 – Biology

I took some introductory biology units through UWA extension, with an interest in applying computing to problems involving biological sciences; moving to Sydney to start a PhD put an end to this.

PhD – 1998 to 2006 – Natural Language Processing

In 1998, I received a scholarship to work on a PhD with the Natural Language Processing group within the Microsoft Research Initiative at Macquarie University. Work in this group focused on shallow techniques for NLP – particularly in the area of text generation. I ended up choosing to look at the resolution of associative anaphora within the context of knowledge acquisition and text summarisation.

Unfortunately, the group folded at the end of my first year, and although I continued on as a student within the computing department at Macquarie, I did not manage to complete work on my thesis.

Gap – Illness and Depression

At the end of my time in Sydney, I was depressed and in fairly poor health. Nothing was ever resolved as to what was wrong with me, but I ended up abandoning work on my thesis and came back to Perth. This lasted for almost a decade, with me doing small projects and maintenance work on the software that I wrote for ATTA, before COVID arrived and I started to regain my interest in life. Since then, I have worked to regain some skills that might be useful in finding work, probably in the IT industry.

TAFE – Infection Control

Towards the end of the COVID epidemic, it seemed like if I were to get any jobs where I needed to attend in person then it would be useful to know something about infection control. There were free online courses on offer through TAFE, and I took one as both a means to prepare for work in a world where COVID was still a major issue, and to get myself back into shape to study and work.

TAFE – Certificate IV in IT (Programming)

I am currently nearing the end of working on a Cert-IV course in IT at North Metroplitan TAFE. It has been interesting.

Work

Timetable Software (Volunteer)

The year before I started at UWA, I wrote some software for the faculty of science to help allocating students to workshop sessions in a way that would not conflict with any of their classes.

Programmer at Jarvis Software

Employed by Jarvis Software as a part-time programmer for work on the Jarvis Education platform and on the Dealer Administration System for Seal Corp.

Clinical Immunology

Helped a group working on DNA sequencing in the Department of Clinical Immunology at UWA – which operated out of Royal Perth Hospital. Most of my time was spent working on tools related to multi-locus DNA sequencing for use in tasks such as determining compatability for transplants and tracking mutations in HIV.

Economics

Worked on software for a PhD student in Economics and Commerce to help extract structured information from plain text reports to the Australian Stock Exchenge.

Timing Software

Created AttaTime: timekeeping software used in various forms by the Australian Time Trials Association from 1992 until 2025.

Linguistics – Aboriginal Languages Database

Created a database and simple web-interface to assist Alan Dench in some research on adapting techniques to reconstructing the original forms of words from lost languages based on records of people not speakers of the language.

Dunmore Lang College – 2008 to 2010 – Computing Tutor

My first three years at Macquarie, I stayed at one of the residential colleges on campus. During that time I offset my living expenses by working as a computing tutor / administrator for the student network.

Tutoring at Macquarie

During my time at Macquarie, I worked as a lab demonstrator for a number of introductory programming units and a couple of higher-level courses in logic programming and NLP.

Hobbies

Reading

I'm not sure if this qualifies as a hobby or a vice, but I enjoy reading. Mostly fiction, but I can get sidetracked onto just about any topic. The availability of reading material on the internet has not ben good for me.

Walking

I'm no longer up to the long gym sessions or bike rides that I used to do while an undergraduate and at Macquarie. Now that I am feeling a bit healthier, I have taken up daily brisk walks as a way to get back into shape.

Cycling - Old

I used to do a fair amount of cycling for pleasure, as well as using a bicycle as my main form of transport. I rode with ATTA for a while before getting caught up with timekeeping, and rode with a club in Sydney. This is something that I'd like to get back to.

Horse Riding – Old

I used to do a fair bit of horse-riding before my move to Sydney. Nothing too competitive, a couple of unofficial one-day events, some dressage, and some showjumping. I haven't looked at this in decades, though.

Programming

I actually enjoy programming for fun. Usually silly projects, like writing a slide viewer in zsh that displays images under the kitty terminal emulator or writing an extensible programming language with a compiler and intermediate language interpreter written in javascript. These are mostly silly things, and not worth releasing to the public.

Since starting at TAFE, I have also developed a fondness for playing around with coding environments like Jupyter Notebooks and Polyglot Notebooks. I would like to spend more time contributing to open-source projects, and intend to when I have more time.
